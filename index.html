The short version

Here is what went wrong.

The pattern across personal vehicle categories:

  • Proprietary batteries built into the frame — sourced only through one channel
  • Controllers with plugs nobody else makes, firmware nobody else can access
  • Dealer networks that won't touch off-brand parts or anything they can't source
  • A product that becomes a brick when the company folds
  • One option at one price — designed for their revenue model, not your life

Sit down. Go anywhere. On parts anyone can source and anyone can fix.

ParaBoards uses bike parts, skateboard parts, scooter parts — available from common vendors, not through us. Anyone inclined can work on it. Anyone who wants to build on the platform can. If we close tomorrow, your ride still works.

The argument

The gap is real. The parts to fill it already exist.

Micromobility proved the price argument wrong.

E-bikes start around $1,000 (about $1,000). Electric scooters under $500 (about $500). The technology for affordable outdoor personal transport exists. The supply chains exist. A capable sit-down vehicle at a reasonable price is not technically impossible. It just hasn't been built.

Proprietary lock-in is a business model, not a technical requirement.

E-bike companies built beautiful products and then folded, leaving riders with bricks because the battery was custom and the supplier was gone. Proprietary plugs, closed firmware, dealer-only parts — it works until the company stops working. ParaBoards uses standardized parts from the bike, skateboard, and scooter supply chains. You can order a replacement today.

The sit-down category was left behind. Not for technical reasons.

Every other personal vehicle category got cheaper, faster, more capable. The seated option didn't follow. ParaBoards is designed for the life you want to live — not the one the market decided you get.
