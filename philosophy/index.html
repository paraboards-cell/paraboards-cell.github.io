Philosophy

The industry produces bad outcomes for predictable reasons. Here is what different looks like.

Mobility devices are expensive, hard to fix, and designed for insurance approval rather than actual use. These aren't accidents. They're the result of a system structured around vendor lock-in, low volume, and a captive user base. ParaBoards is designed against all of it.

The argument, in full

This page will contain the complete philosophy document: how the mobility device industry operates, why it produces the outcomes it does, what a different model looks like, and how ParaBoards implements it. The three-part structure follows: here is the pattern, here is the proof it isn't necessary, here is what we built instead.

The thesis

"Everything we do is not that way."

Electric scooters and e-bikes are already affordable consumer products. The technology exists. The supply chains exist. The manufacturing capacity exists. The only reason mobility devices cost ten times as much and break twice as often is that nobody built the right system. That's the gap ParaBoards occupies.
